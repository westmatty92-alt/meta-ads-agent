# Marketing Skills — Ad Builder Enhancement
# Based on whyashthakker/agent-skills-marketing

## ICP Definition Framework
Before writing any ad, define the Ideal Customer Profile:
- Demographics: age, gender, income, location, job
- Psychographics: values, beliefs, lifestyle, identity
- Behavioral: buying triggers, objections, decision process
- Pain intensity: how badly do they feel this problem?
- Language: exact words they use to describe their problem

## Positioning Matrix
For every ad, answer these before writing:
- What category does this product compete in?
- What is the ONE thing we do better than everyone else?
- What is the specific enemy (the thing we replace or defeat)?
- What proof do we have that our claim is true?
- What does the customer lose if they don't act?

## Pain Point Scanning Framework
Rank pain points by:
1. Frequency — how often does this pain occur?
2. Intensity — how much does it hurt (1-10)?
3. Urgency — do they need to solve it now?
4. Willingness to pay — will they spend money to fix it?
Only write ads targeting pain points that score 7+ on intensity.

## Competitive Ad Analysis
Before writing, ask:
- What are competitors saying? (angle, offer, hook)
- What are they NOT saying? (the gap = your opportunity)
- What customer fears are they ignoring?
- What desires are they leaving unaddressed?
Write into the gap — own what competitors ignore.

## Ad Copy Framework (AIDA + Fear/Desire)
Structure every ad:
1. HOOK — interrupt the scroll (lead with fear OR desire, never both)
2. AGITATE — make the pain feel real and specific
3. BRIDGE — introduce the solution as the only logical answer
4. PROOF — one specific credibility signal
5. CTA — single clear action with urgency

## Quality Gates (check before every ad)
- [ ] Does the hook stop a thumb mid-scroll?
- [ ] Is the pain/desire specific to this segment (not generic)?
- [ ] Does it sound like a human, not a brand?
- [ ] Is there one clear CTA?
- [ ] Would this person recognize themselves in this ad?
- [ ] Does it differentiate from what competitors are saying?
